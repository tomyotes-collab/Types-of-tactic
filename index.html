Types of Tactics

Three families of tactics, two guiding principles — and a sorting challenge at the end.

The three families
Content & Creative

What you say & how you say it

Storytelling, emotional appeals, educational posts, behind-the-scenes, problem-solving content. Rich media (photos, video) consistently outperforms plain text.

📸 Nike "You Can't Stop Us" split-screen video
Platform-Specific

Where you show up

Each platform has its own culture, algorithm, and audience expectation. The same message lands differently on TikTok vs LinkedIn vs Facebook.

💼 LinkedIn thought-leadership articles for B2B
Format-Specific

The container for your message

Short video, carousel, UGC campaign, AR filter, influencer collab — the format shapes how audiences engage, share, and remember your message.

🎥 Red Bull Stratos — live-streamed freefall UGC moment
Two core principles (Sutherland, 2024)
75%

Stop Selling, Start Helping

The vast majority of your tactics should give value — share knowledge, solve problems, build trust — before any product mention. Think of it as earning the right to sell.

💡 Duolingo's TikTok — entertains first, never hard-sells
25%

Quality Storytelling

When you do promote, lead with a real human story. Effective stories focus on the problem the product solves — and what solving it means to someone's life, not just their wallet.

❤️ Dove "Real Beauty Sketches" — stories, not specs
